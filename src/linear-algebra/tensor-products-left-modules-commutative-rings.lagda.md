## Idea

The
{{#concept "tensor product" Disambiguation="of two left modules over a commutative ring" Agda=tensor-product-left-module-Commutative-Ring}}
of two [left modules](linear-algebra.left-modules-commutative-rings.md) `M` and
`N` over a [commutative ring](commutative-algebra.commutative-rings.md) `R` is a
left module `M ⊗ N` over `R` equipped with a
[bilinear map](linear-algebra.bilinear-maps-left-modules-commutative-rings.md)
`i : M → N → M ⊗ N` satisfying the universal property that for any left module
`P` over `R` and any bilinear map `b : M → N → P`, `b` factors uniquely into a
composition `m ∘ i` where `m : M ⊗ N → P` is a
[linear map](linear-algebra.linear-maps-left-modules-commutative-rings.md).

The standard construction of the tensor product, used here, is the
[quotient](universal-algebra.quotients-algebras.md) of the
[free](universal-algebra.freely-generated-algebras.md) left module over `R`
generated by `M × N` by an appropriate
[congruence](universal-algebra.congruences.md) to ensure the mapping is
bilinear.
